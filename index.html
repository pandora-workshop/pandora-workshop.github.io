Welcome

About the workshop

PANDORA is envisioned as a forum for discussing how natural language processing systems can be made more reliable, interpretable, and useful in real-world settings. We welcome interdisciplinary perspectives spanning modeling, evaluation, human-computer interaction, ethics, safety, and deployment.

The workshop will feature archival paper presentations, invited talks, and structured discussion sessions focused on open challenges in trustworthy NLP. We especially encourage submissions that combine technical novelty with clear practical relevance.

Call for Papers

Topics of interest

We invite original research papers, position papers, and system demonstrations related to trustworthy and deployable NLP. Relevant topics include, but are not limited to:

  • Robustness, calibration, and uncertainty estimation for language models
  • Evaluation methodologies for reliability, fairness, and safety
  • Interpretability, transparency, and explanation for NLP systems
  • Human-centered evaluation and participatory design
  • Responsible deployment in education, health, policy, and industry
  • Data governance, documentation, and reproducibility practices

Submission categories

  • Long papers: up to 8 pages of content, plus references
  • Short papers: up to 4 pages of content, plus references
  • Non-archival extended abstracts for demos or discussion papers

Accepted papers will be presented as talks or posters, depending on program constraints and the nature of the contribution.

Author Guidelines

Formatting and reviewing

Formatting

Submissions should follow the official EMNLP 2026 formatting guidelines. Anonymous review requirements will apply unless the conference announces a different policy for workshops.

Review criteria

We will evaluate submissions for originality, technical soundness, clarity, relevance to the workshop theme, and expected value to the community.

Artifact expectations

Authors are encouraged to share code, data statements, and model cards whenever possible to improve reproducibility and downstream usability.

Program

Planned format

Invited talks

Leaders from academia and industry will speak on trustworthy NLP deployment.

Paper sessions

Accepted long and short papers will be grouped into thematic presentation blocks.

Panel and discussion

A collaborative session will focus on open problems, benchmarks, and community needs.
